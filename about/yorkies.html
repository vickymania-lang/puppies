Origins of the Yorkie

The Yorkshire Terrier’s history dates back to the 19th century in Yorkshire, England, where they were bred for their agility and determination in catching vermin. Despite their small size, Yorkshire Terriers, or Yorkies, are known for their big hearts and spunky attitudes, making them delightful pets. The Yorkshire Terrier’s coat, characterized by its silky texture, adds to their charm. Whether you are looking for a Yorkshire Terrier puppy or considering bringing a Yorkshire Terrier into your home, their rich history and distinctive coat make them a unique and cherished companion. Discover the joy of having a Yorkshire Terrier, a breed celebrated for its agility, determination, and endearing qualities.

Characteristics and Temperament


Yorkshire Terriers, or Yorkies, are characterized by their tiny stature, beautiful silky coats, and a lively, confident demeanor. Their silky coat length, often compared to human hair, adds to their unique charm. Despite their small size, Yorkies are known for their courage, making them excellent watchdogs.

Their intelligent nature requires mental stimulation, and their playful and affectionate disposition makes them great companions for families, singles, or seniors. Their manageable energy level also makes them suitable for city dwellers and apartment living. However, their beautiful coats require regular grooming and brushing to maintain their appearance. Yorkies, with their brave and spirited personality, bring joy and companionship to any home, making them an ideal choice for those seeking a small, intelligent, and affectionate canine companion.
